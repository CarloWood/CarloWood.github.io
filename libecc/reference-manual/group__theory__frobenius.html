Theory: The Frobenius map
Let and be fields.  A field homomorphism is a function : such that:
  1. (a + b) = (a) + (b) for all a, b
  2. (a b) = (a) (b) for all a, b
  3. (1) = 1, (0) = 0
It can be proven that a homomorphism is injective.  If is also surjective then the map is obviously a bijection and is in fact an automorphism.  An automorphism is just an isomorphism of a system of objects onto itself.In our case, the Frobenius map of the elements of onto itself will turn out to be surjective and therefore be an automorphism: an isomorphism of the field onto itself.The Frobenius homomorphism is a map named after the mathematician Frobenius, and its just 'cause he was born first that it has his name because all he did was realize that for a field with characteristic p we have (a + b)p = ap + bp.  I am not going to spill any more words on that, except that in our case p = 2 and if by now you can't even dream that (a + b)2 = a2 + b2 then don't you think it will be easier for you start reading at the beginning of all of this?Indeed, the Frobenius homomorphism for our field is defined as:
