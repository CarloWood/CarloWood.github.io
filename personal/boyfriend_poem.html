This poem was first written for a boy called "Ting". That wasn't his real name, but he liked to be called like that and everyone did. He was from Thailand and really touched my heart. Unfortunately, he lied to me that he didn't have a boyfriend and we broke up after about three weeks when he finally told me "because he was falling in love with me too" and he didn't want that because "he already had a boyfriend". Of course, the poem was written before he told me that (over the phone).

The original poem was slightly different, and personally I like the original more.

Here's the original poem, devoted to Ting:

If I could be living thousands of years,
and visit all planets, to see their moon.
Stayed young forever and shed no tears,
listen to whales, and understand their tune;

If I could make music that made everyone cry
and birds would be listening when I start to sing.
If I could see God - when I finally die,
then instead I will be, the boyfriend of Ting.

In total it took me eight hours to write these eight lines. The main reason being that I wanted the rhythm to be perfect (too). There should be a short pause after "living", "forever", "music" and after every comma. A long(er) pause comes after "tune", "cry" and "God". "The boyfriend of Ting" needs to be read a little bit slower then the rest, as a conclusion. The melody of this poem is at least as important but it's hard to describe that in text. However, note that the tone should go down at "years", "tears", "sing", "die" and "Ting", while the tone should go up on "living", "thousands", "all" and "music". The tone of other words also should go up and down but a little less then these; if you want then you can try it out until you find what sounds right.

P.S. Note that when I could actually choose between seeing God when I die, and having any boy as boyfriend now - then I'd like to see God (If the choice was seeing God or being with my boyfriend, after we both died, then I'd have second thoughts though).
